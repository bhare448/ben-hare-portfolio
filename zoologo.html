Zoo Logo

Zoo Logo designed for my graphic design class.

Project Overview

This project was made in my "Digital Graphic Design" class and was meant to teach us the basics of Photoshop and Illustrator and test our artistic abilities. The goal of this project was to create a new logo for a zoo of our choosing and create two pieces of merchandise with our logo on it. I chose to make a logo for the Lake Tobias Wildlife Park, which is a zoo I used to visit a lot growing up.

Design Process

Ideation:

  • I decided pretty quickly that I wanted to create a logo for Lake Tobias, but I was not sure which animal I wanted to include as the main focus of the logo. Most advertising for Lake Tobias uses a giraffe to promote their "Giraffe Encounter" attraction, but another big attraction of theirs is the "Safari Tour", which includes a variety of animals like deer, bison, and antelopes.
  • I eventually decided on a reindeer as the main focus because I thought that it captured the serenity of the wildlife park well and the big antlers could be easily recognized from a distance.

Research:

  • Looking at other zoo logos, I noticed that many follow a similar design by including a recognizable animal, usually one of their main attractions and make use of negative space.
  • These logos served as a helpful reference when creating my own logo.

Brainstorming:

  • My professor emphasized that when creating an effective logo, the use of negative space is very important because it makes the logo stand out. I was wondering how I would be able to incorporate negative space into my logo and eventually decided on a front-facing deer head with the text in the middle.
  • Deciding which font to use for the text was difficult because I wanted to make it fit the theme of the zoo while still being readable from a distance. I also wanted to pick a color that fit the branding.
  • For my other two products, I decided to put the logo on a T-shirt and a business card.

Prototype:

  • For the prototype, I used Illustrator to trace over a deer head and darkened it to make it into a silhouette. I ended up deciding on a font and color that was very similar to their official logo.
  • After the logo was done, I wanted to create a sleek T-shirt design that went beyond just putting the logo on the front. I decided to have the antlers wrap around the side of the shirt to make it more unique and added a simplified logo on the back. To make the logo fit on a business card, I had to move the location of the text, but still kept the same recognition.

Final Outcome

I am happy with the way that the final product turned out and I think this was a very good introduction to the Photoshop and Illustrator softwares, which I know are used frequently in professional settings. Since this project, I have continued honing my skills in both of these softwares and feel confident that I can use them effectively in most professional scenarios.
